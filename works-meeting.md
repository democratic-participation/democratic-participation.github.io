# Works Meeting: Election of an Election Committee

<div class="info-box warning">

## Important: What This Meeting Is About

This meeting is **NOT** a vote on whether there will be a works council. Instead, it is a formal process to elect an election committee that will then organize the actual works council elections.

</div>

<div class="section-highlight current-status">

## Meeting Information

**Date:** To be announced.  
**Purpose:** Election of an election committee to prepare works council elections

</div>

<div class="info-box">

### Meeting Agenda

1. **Information** on the purpose and procedure of the meeting
2. **Election** of a chairman of the meeting and a keeper of the minutes  
3. **Election** of an election committee

</div>

<div class="section-highlight process-steps">

## Legal Framework

The invitation to this works meeting sets the works council election process in motion according to **ยง17 BetrVG** (Works Constitution Act).

### How the Election Committee is Formed

- If at least **3 employees** stand for election
- And **three votes** are cast for each of these employees
- Then the election committee is **automatically formed**

This is a formal legal process with no room for interpretation.

</div>

## Who Can Participate?

**Eligible to vote:**
- All employees of the German entity
- **Excluding:** employers and senior executives ("leitende Angestellte")
- **Must be present** at the meeting (no remote voting)

**Eligible to stand for election committee:**
- All employees of the German entity entitled to vote
- Who have been employed by the company for **6+ months**
- And are at least **18 years old**

## What Happens After the Election Committee is Formed?

The elected election committee will:
- Prepare and implement the works council election
- Follow strict legal regulations (BetrVG and Election Regulation)
- Have very limited decision-making power
- Must act according to the law to ensure the election cannot be contested

## Key Points to Remember

### Timeline
- **One week minimum** notice period required by law
- We aim to provide employees with at least **three weeks** time for travel arrangements
- The meeting focuses only on electing the election committee
- Further information about works council elections will be provided by the elected committee

### Attendance
- Only employees **physically present** can vote
- Not attending does **not** count as abstention
- The law requires only a majority of those present

### Candidates
- Candidates cannot introduce themselves until the election meeting
- The best candidates should be able to read, understand, and apply the relevant laws
- Individual political views should play a minimal role

## No Fundamental Debate

There will be **no fundamental discussion** about whether to have a works council. The agenda is fixed and focuses solely on the election process.

## Background

This initiative was started by **a group of ten employees** who:
- Act independently without management involvement
- Provide this information voluntarily in their spare time
- Are conducting this process for the first time
